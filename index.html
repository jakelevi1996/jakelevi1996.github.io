About

I'm a PhD student on the AIMS CDT supervised by Professor Mark van der Wilk in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Oxford.

I research computer models of neural networks. In recent decades, ML research has succeeded in understanding how to train AI models that are highly intelligent, assuming access to extremely huge datasets and expensive GPU servers. But ML research has failed (so far) at developing AI models that can learn as efficiently as humans can learn, in terms of both memory and data consumption.

The ultimate goal of my research is to understand how to design AI models that can learn any task at least as efficiently as a human could learn it. Such an understanding would be useful in practise, and would also deepen our scientific understanding of intelligence, which would therefore deepen our understanding of ourselves.

From an evolutionary perspective, the efficiency with which we learn is a fundamentally important component of natural intelligence, that is necessary for survival in the wild. Understanding how to learn efficiently from natural data is fundamentally absent from our understanding of intelligence. And I think that understanding intelligence may help us to appreciate its brilliance.

ADHD

In July 2024 I found out I have ADHD. ADHD has affected me significantly at some times in my life, and less so at other times. I am most productive when I have the freedom to pursue what I am most passionate about, in the way that works best for me. I'm sure this is true for everyone to an extent, but relatively I can struggle much more when I don't have that freedom. I'm also frequently late (which really bothers me), and sometimes struggle with deadlines (for example, staying up all night before a presentation in order to finish making the slides, even though I was planning to make them a week in advance).

But there are advantages of having ADHD. For example, in order to complete tasks that other people find easy, I often have to find a different way of doing things that works for me. This is often slower/more difficult in the short-term, but can put me at a huge advantage in the long-term (a bit like training with a weight vest). The dependence of my productivity on my interests also forces me into positions where I am pursuing my interests (at the expense of a higher salary and/or an easier life), so I don't waste time that could otherwise be spent pursuing my interests. I love doing a PhD because I have a lot of freedom to pursue the ideas I am most passionate about, and to think differently.
